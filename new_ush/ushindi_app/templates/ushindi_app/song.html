A Testimony of Brenda Museve a Current Child

My name is Brenda Museve. I am 13 years old in Grade six. I want to tell you a testimony about my life. Life with my parents was very good and pleasant together with siblings. My dad died in 2018 causing a lot of stress to us and our mother. We were rejected by close relatives and wanted to force us from our homes, were it not the intervention of the local government they would have succeeded. One day a well-wisher presented our case to daddy Justus and mummy Jane Amunga and by good luck we were accepted into Ushindi Children’s Home. Ushindi has offered a loving opportunity for new friends, a place to rejoice, provided education, balanced diet, clothes, and shelter. We thank Daddy and Mommy for the loving and caring for us. May God bless them, our sponsors, and staff. When I grow up, I would like to be a pilot.

A Testimony of Timothy Musonga an Alumni

My name is Timothy Musonga, am an orphan. I lost my both parents at my little age of 3yrs. It was painful and sorrowful to loose the most beloved parents that age. My elder sister and I, we had no one to call dad nor mom. After the burial of my mother who followed after my father's burial in a short moment , my uncle took us into his home since we had no where to go to. We persevered through hardship since my uncle had no job nor source of income. In a life span he generally began to hate us naturally despite that we did nothing wrong. He asked his family to isolate from us since we are cursed . We wished our parents could be with us since we couldn't find love and care at that little age. One fine morning my aunt pay a visit to her brother's house and she was not pleased with how they mistreated us. We spoke to her and promised as she is going to take as to a children's home (orphanage). "Thanks to mama Jane Amaunga and the late Bishop Justus Amunga together with our sponsors from AOO at large.You loved and cared for us, gave us hope in life since we were hopeless, made us know God's expectations in life and also took us to school to acquire knowledge. They generally provided us all the basic need my almighty God blessings shower abundantly upon them." After my school, I decided to go back home and stay on my father's land since it was the only inheritance left for me. I was shocked to find that the property I was left with had already been sold to someone else by my uncle without my consent. I went ahead to those in authority to assist me in claiming it back but unfortunately everything was in vein. Instead my uncle made plans to bring distraction into my life. I began to suffer from headache till the time It developed to psychological problem. All-over a sudden I went mad for 3yrs, I met a good friend of mine, a brother too whom we grew up together at ushindi children home. He took me to a psychiatric clinic and I was attended on. By now sincerely speaking it's God's grace, I have been gradually changing and am greatly appreciating the good hand of God over my life. I will never late my past to ruin my future but its my prayer that God's will is going to lead me and I am going to have my on property in Jesus name.
